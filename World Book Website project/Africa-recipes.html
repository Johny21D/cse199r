Moroccan Lamb Tagine

Region: North Africa

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Lamb shoulder, cubed
  • 2 Onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Dried apricots
  • 1 tsp each: Ground ginger, Cinnamon, Turmeric, Cumin
  • 500ml Beef or vegetable stock

Instructions

  1. Brown the lamb in a heavy-bottomed pot with oil over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until soft.
  3. Stir in all the spices for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Pour in the stock and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook on low heat for 1.5 hours.
  5. Add apricots and cook for another 30 minutes until the meat is tender.

Nigerian Jollof Rice

Region: West Africa

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 Red bell peppers & 3 Scotch bonnets (blended)
  • 5 Tomatoes (blended)
  • 1/2 cup Vegetable oil
  • 2 Onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp Tomato paste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil and fry sliced onions. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the blended pepper/tomato mix. Cook until the oil rises to the top.
  3. Add seasonings and 2 cups of stock. Bring to a boil.
  4. Stir in washed rice. Cover tightly with foil and a lid to trap steam.
  5. Cook on very low heat for 30 minutes until rice is fluffy.

Ethiopian Misir Wat

Region: East Africa

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Red lentils, rinsed
  • 2 tbsp Berbere spice blend
  • 1 large Onion, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp Niter Kibbeh (spiced butter) or oil
  • 1 tsp Garlic-ginger paste

Instructions

  1. Dry-fry onions until moisture is gone, then add butter/oil.
  2. Stir in Berbere spice and cook for 2 minutes to toast the spices.
  3. Add garlic-ginger paste and lentils.
  4. Add 2 cups of water, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes until soft.
  5. Salt to taste and serve warm with Injera.

South African Bobotie

Region: Southern Africa

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Ground beef or lamb
  • 2 slices White bread (soaked in milk)
  • 2 Onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp Curry powder & 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 3 tbsp Chutney (ideally Mrs. Ball's Peach Chutney)
  • 1/4 cup Sultanas or raisins
  • For the topping: 2 Eggs & 1 cup Milk, 2 Bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Sauté onions in a pan until soft, then add the ground meat and brown it thoroughly.
  2. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, chutney, raisins, and the soaked bread (squeeze out excess milk first). Mix well.
  3. Transfer the meat mixture into a greased baking dish and press it down firmly.
  4. Whisk the eggs and milk together, then pour the mixture over the meat. Tuck the bay leaves into the top.
  5. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 30 minutes, or until the custard topping is set and golden brown. Serve with yellow rice.
