Culinary Wonders of South America

Colombian Arepas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Cornmeal (P.A.N.)
  • 2 ½ cups Warm water
  • 1 tsp Salt & 1 tbsp Oil

Instructions:

  1. Mix water, salt, and oil. Add cornmeal.
  2. Shape into discs.
  3. Sear for 5-7 mins per side.

Peruvian Ceviche

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb White fish (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup Lime juice
  • Red onion & Habanero

Instructions:

  1. Season fish with salt/chili.
  2. Add onions and lime juice.
  3. Marinate 5–10 mins and serve.

Brazilian Brigadeiros

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz Condensed milk
  • 1 tbsp Butter
  • 3 tbsp Cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. Cook ingredients until thick.
  2. Cool on a greased plate.
  3. Roll into balls and sprinkle.

Peruvian Ceviche

Region: South America (Peru)

Ingredients

  • 500g Fresh white fish (Sea Bass or Tilapia)
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Lime juice
  • 1 Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Fresh Cilantro and Aji Limo (chili)
  • Served with: Sweet potato and Corn (Choclo)

Instructions

  1. Cut fish into small cubes and place in a glass bowl.
  2. Cover with lime juice, salt, and chili; let marinate for 10-15 mins.
  3. Toss in red onions and chopped cilantro.
  4. Serve immediately while the fish is firm and "cooked" by the acid.

Brazilian Picanha Steak

Region: South America (Brazil)

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg Picanha (Sirloin Cap)
  • Coarse Sea Salt (Sal Grosso)
  • Olive oil (optional)
  • Chimichurri sauce (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Score the fat cap in a diamond pattern (don't cut the meat).
  2. Rub generously with coarse sea salt.
  3. Skewer the meat in a "C" shape or cut into thick steaks.
  4. Grill over high heat, fat-side up first, until medium-rare.

Stuffed Arepas

Region: South America

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Pre-cooked cornmeal (Masarepa)
  • 2.5 cups Warm water
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • Filling: Shredded beef, Black beans, or Cheese

Instructions

  1. Mix cornmeal, salt, and water to form a smooth dough.
  2. Form into flat discs about 1 inch thick.
  3. Grill or pan-fry for 5 minutes per side until a crust forms.
  4. Slice open halfway and stuff with your favorite fillings.

Argentine Empanadas

Region: South America (Argentina)

Ingredients

  • Empanada dough discs (Tapas)
  • 500g Ground beef
  • Cumin, Paprika, and Green onions
  • Hard-boiled eggs and Green olives

Instructions

  1. Sauté beef with spices and onions; let cool.
  2. Place a spoonful of meat, an olive, and a slice of egg on each disc.
  3. Fold and seal with a "repulgue" (braided edge).
  4. Bake at 200°C for 20 minutes until golden brown.

Chilean Curanto en Hoyo

Region: Southern Chile

Ingredients

  • Mixed Shellfish (Clams, Mussels)
  • Chicken pieces & Smoked Pork
  • Potatoes & Milcao (Potato pancakes)
  • Large Nalca (Rhubarb) or Cabbage leaves

Instructions

  1. Dig a pit and heat stones until glowing red.
  2. Layer the ingredients: Shellfish first, then meat, then potatoes.
  3. Cover with leaves and wet sacks to trap the steam.
  4. Cook for 1-2 hours; the shellfish juices flavor everything below.

Traditional Māori Hāngī

Region: Oceania (New Zealand)

Ingredients

  • Pork, Lamb, and Chicken
  • Kumara (Sweet Potato)
  • Pumpkin and Cabbage
  • Stuffing (Breadcrumbs and Herbs)

Instructions

  1. Heat volcanic stones in a pit until white-hot.
  2. Place food in wire baskets lined with cabbage leaves.
  3. Lower baskets into the pit and cover with wet earth to seal.
  4. Slow-cook for 3-4 hours until the meat falls off the bone.

Chakalaka & Pap

Region: Southern Africa

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Maize meal (for Pap)
  • Chakalaka: Carrots, Peppers, Onions, Chilies
  • 1 can Baked beans
  • Curry powder and Ginger/Garlic

Instructions

  1. Boil water and slowly whisk in maize meal to make a thick porridge (Pap).
  2. Sauté vegetables with curry powder and ginger.
  3. Stir in baked beans and simmer until the relish is thick.
  4. Serve the cold/warm Chakalaka over the hot, buttery Pap.

Patagonian Lamb Asado

Region: Southern Argentina

Ingredients

  • 1 Whole lamb (butterflied)
  • Salmuera (Brine): Water, Salt, Garlic, Rosemary
  • Hardwood for the fire

Instructions

  1. Mount the lamb on an iron cross (Asador).
  2. Position it at an angle over a bed of glowing wood embers.
  3. Baste the meat every 30 minutes with the Salmuera brine.
  4. Cook slowly for 4-5 hours, rotating once, until the skin is crackling.
